# A Brief History of NetHack

NetHack was first released in July 1987 by Mike Stephenson, built upon the foundation of the 1982 game *Hack*. The "Net" in the title refers to its development being coordinated over Usenet by an early decentralised working group known as the DevTeam. 

Written entirely in C, it is structurally a deterministic state machine that tracks thousands of discrete entities across a procedurally generated 24×80 ASCII grid. Mechanically, it is a triumph of memory management and procedural complexity. The primary user interface relies entirely on the keyboard, typically utilising standard `hjklyubn` directional keys.

Thematically, however, the DevTeam inexplicably chose to dress this mathematical engine in the sweeping, melodramatic trappings of high fantasy. The nominal objective is to descend through 50-odd levels of the treacherous 'Gehennom' to retrieve the legendary 'Amulet of Yendor' for a demanding deity. In practice, this means you will watch a brave little `@` symbol bravely navigate a matrix of full stops, only to be unceremoniously terminated because it inadvertently consumed an out-of-date lizard corpse or tripped over a lowercase `d` (which we are reliably informed is a dog). 

It is a masterpiece of software engineering, where the most glorious, heart-stopping adventures are reduced to a blinking cursor updating an attribute struct.
